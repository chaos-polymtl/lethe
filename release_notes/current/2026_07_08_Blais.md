## [Master] - 2026/07/08

### Added

- MAJOR A new `impinging_jet_mixer` mesh lethe grid generator was added (selectable through the `type = lethe` parameter). It builds a fully hexahedral, conforming mesh for an impinging-jet mixer: a vertical cylindrical mixing chamber closed at the top by a hemispherical dome, a conical reduction followed by a straight outlet pipe below the chamber, and two opposing horizontal inlet pipes entering the chamber side wall from `+x` and `-x`. Because a round pipe meeting the curved chamber wall is a saddle intersection that cannot be produced by merging library primitives, each inlet pipe is instead grown out of the refined chamber wall itself, so the junction stays watertight and conforming. The two inlets share a common axis and the mesh is datumed so that axis lies on the `z = 0` (impingement) plane. The `grid arguments` accepts either an empty string (default dimensions) or exactly eight colon-separated dimensions in meters, in the order `R_chamber : r_inlet : r_outlet : H_chamber : L_cone : L_outlet : L_inlet : z_inlet`; the discretisation is fixed internally and not exposed. Boundary IDs are 0 for the `+x` inlet, 1 for the `-x` inlet, 2 for the outlet, and 3 for all walls. This mesh will be featured in a future example.
