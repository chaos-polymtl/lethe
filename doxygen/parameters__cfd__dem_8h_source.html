69 /**
70 * @enum DragCoupling
71 * @brief Defines the numerical coupling strategy used for drag force computation between fluid and particles.
72 *
73 * This enumeration specifies how the drag interaction term is treated in time
74 * integration schemes for coupled CFD-DEM or multiphase simulations. The
75 * choice of coupling affects both stability and computational cost. The
76 * fully_implicit and fully_explicit schemes are named this way since explicit
77 * is a reserved keyword in C++. Thus, we use fully_implicit and
78 * fully_explicit to ensure a coherent terminology.
79 *
80 * - **fully_implicit**:
81 * The drag force is evaluated using the current (implicit) fluid
82 * velocities and previous (explicit) particle velocity. The resulting
83 * drag is applied to both the particles and the fluid.
84 * Provides maximum stability, but requires more Newton iterations.
85 *
86 * - **semi_implicit**:
87 * The drag force on the particles is computed using the previous velocities
88 * of both the particles and the fluid. When transferring momentum to the
89 * fluid, the previous fluid velocity is used to evaluate the momentum
90 * transfer coefficient, while the current fluid velocity is used to
91 * calculate relative velocity between the particle and the fluid. This
92 * provides extensive stability, but for larger time step may lead to a
93 * slight violation of Newton's third law since the drag applied on the
94 * fluid is not strictly equal to the drag applied to the particles.
95 *
96 * - **fully_explicit**:
97 * The drag is evaluated entirely using known quantities from the previous
98 * time step. The outcome conditionally stable and may require very small
99 * coupling time step when the density of the fluid is much lower than the
100 * density of the particles
101 *
102 */
103 enum class DragCoupling
104 {
105 /// Fully implicit drag coupling
106 fully_implicit,
107 /// Semi-implicit drag coupling
108 semi_implicit,
109 /// Explicit drag coupling
110 fully_explicit
111 };
