47 /**
48 * @enum DragCoupling
49 * @brief Defines the numerical coupling strategy used for drag force computation between fluid and particles.
50 *
51 * This enumeration specifies how the drag interaction term is treated in time
52 * integration schemes for coupled CFD-DEM or multiphase simulations. The
53 * choice of coupling affects both stability and computational cost. The
54 * fully_implicit and fully_explicit schemes are named this way since explicit
55 * is a reserved keyword in C++. Thus, we use fully_implicit and
56 * fully_explicit to ensure a coherent terminology.
57 *
58 * - **fully_implicit**:
59 * The drag force is evaluated using the current (implicit) fluid
60 * velocities and previous (explicit) particle velocity. The resulting
61 * drag is applied to both the particles and the fluid.
62 * Provides maximum stability, but requires more Newton iterations.
63 *
64 * - **semi_implicit**:
65 * The drag force on the particles is computed using the previous velocities
66 * of both the particles and the fluid. When transferring momentum to the
67 * fluid, the previous fluid velocity is used to evaluate the momentum
68 * transfer coefficient, while the current fluid velocity is used to
69 * calculate relative velocity between the particle and the fluid. This
70 * provides extensive stability, but for larger time-step may lead to a
71 * slight violation of Newton's third law since the drag applied on the
72 * fluid is not strictly equal to the drag applied to the particles.
73 *
74 * - **fully_explicit**:
75 * The drag is evaluated entirely using known quantities from the previous
76 * time step. The outcome conditionally stable and may require very small
77 * coupling time-step when the density of the fluid is much lower than the
78 * density of the particles
79 *
80 */
81 enum class DragCoupling
82 {
83 fully_implicit,
84 semi_implicit,
85 fully_explicit
86 };
