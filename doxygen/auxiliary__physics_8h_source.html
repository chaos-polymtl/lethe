16/**
17 * Au auxiliary physics is defined as a physics that is solved on top of
18 * a core physics, the latter being the Navier-Stokes equations.
19 * Auxiliary physics are simpler physics around which the entire simulation
20 * process does not need to be tailored. Examples of auxiliary physics are
21 * temperature and concentration. Generally, the auxiliary physics does not
22 * affect the core physics. For example, temperature is advected by the fluid
23 * flow, but buoyancy effects are not taken into account.
24 *
25 * The auxiliary physics are managed by the multiphysics interface of Lethe.
26 *
27 * This base class is used to establish all of the routines that an auxiliary
28 * physics must be able to provide to the Multiphysics interface. These elements
29 * are then called at specific moments of a simulation.
30 *
31 * Auxiliary physics are templated by the dimension of the problem and the
32 * vector type that is used to manage their data.
33 *
34 *
35 * Current limitations:
36 *
37 * - Auxiliary physics are currently expected to be used in conjunction with a
38 * flow solver which can provide a velocity field.
39 * - Support for feedback from the auxiliary physics to the core physics is
40 * there but presently not used anywhere
41 * - Support for interaction between auxiliary physics is supported but has not
42 * been tested
43 **/
44template <int dim, typename VectorType>
45class AuxiliaryPhysics : public PhysicsSolver<VectorType>

47public:

49 * @brief AuxiliaryPhysics - Base constructor for Auxiliary physics. At the present

51 * virtual class.

175 */
