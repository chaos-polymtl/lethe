15/**
16 * Au auxiliary physics is defined as a physics that is solved on top of
17 * a core physics, the latter being the Navier-Stokes equations.
18 * Auxiliary physics are simpler physics around which the entire simulation
19 * process does not need to be tailored. Examples of auxiliary physics are
20 * temperature and concentration. Generally, the auxiliary physics does not
21 * affect the core physics. For example, temperature is advected by the fluid
22 * flow, but buoyancy effects are not taken into account.
23 *
24 * The auxiliary physics are managed by the multiphysics interface of Lethe.
25 *
26 * This base class is used to establish all of the routines that an auxiliary
27 * physics must be able to provide to the Multiphysics interface. These elements
28 * are then called at specific moments of a simulation.
29 *
30 * Auxiliary physics are templated by the dimension of the problem and the
31 * vector type that is used to manage their data.
32 *
33 *
34 * Current limitations:
35 *
36 * - Auxiliary physics are currently expected to be used in conjunction with a
37 * flow solver which can provide a velocity field.
38 * - Support for feedback from the auxiliary physics to the core physics is
39 * there but presently not used anywhere
40 * - Support for interaction between auxiliary physics is supported but has not
41 * been tested
42 **/

180 /**
181 * @brief Set up preconditioner. Not used for the auxiliary physics but
