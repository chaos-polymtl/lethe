Detailed Description

Isothermal ideal gas density.

Isothermal ideal gas density assumes that the fluid's density varies according the following state equation:

\(\rho = \rho_\text{ref} + \psi p = \rho_\text{ref} + \frac{1}{R T} \ p\)

where \(\rho_\text{ref}\) is the density of the fluid at the reference state, \(\psi = \frac{1}{R T}\) is the compressibility factor derived from the ideal gas law with \(R= \frac{R_u}{M}\) the specific gas constant (universal gas constant ( \(R_u\)) divided by the molar mass of the gas ( \(M\))) and \(T\) the temperature of the gas, finally, \(p\) is the differential pressure between the reference state and the current state. The model is used for weakly compressible flows when temperature fluctuations' influence on density can be neglected.
