Detailed Description

template<int dim>
class DynamicViscosityPostprocessor< dim >

Compute the dynamic viscosity scalar field of a fluid within a domain.

The dynamic viscosity is defined as \(\mu = \frac{\nu}{\rho_\mathrm{ref}}\) where \(\nu\) is the kinematic viscosity and \(\rho_\mathrm{ref}\) is the density of the fluid at reference state.

The expression for the kinematic viscosity depends on the rheological model of the fluid. See documentation on rheological models.
